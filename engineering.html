Leo

This project involved an in-depth analysis of the hydrodynamic principles behind jellyfish locomotion. We aimed to understand the complex fluid interactions generated by their unique bell pulsation mechanism.

Methodology:

Our approach combined observational studies with Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) simulations. High-speed video footage of live jellyfish (Aurelia aurita) was used to extract kinematic data, which then informed the parameters of our CFD models.

Key Findings & Observations:

  • Successfully modeled the formation and shedding of vortex rings during the jellyfish's swimming cycle.
  • Identified a strong correlation between bell deformation patterns and propulsive efficiency.
  • The study highlighted the sophisticated and highly efficient nature of this seemingly simple form of locomotion.

The insights gained could potentially inform the design of novel bio-inspired underwater propulsion systems.
